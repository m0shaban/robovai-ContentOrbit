**Controlling AI Agent Sprawl: A Growing Concern for CIOs**

As AI adoption accelerates across businesses, a new challenge is emerging: AI agent sprawl. With various business units rapidly adopting generative technologies, corporate networks are becoming increasingly populated with unmonitored and fragmented AI agents. This creates a significant governance blind spot for CIOs managing multi-cloud infrastructures.

The situation echoes the shadow IT issues of the cloud era, but with a crucial difference: AI agents are autonomous and can execute actions without human oversight. This raises serious concerns about security, compliance, and data management.

To mitigate these risks, CIOs need to develop effective governance strategies for AI agent sprawl. This includes implementing monitoring and management tools, establishing clear policies and guidelines for AI adoption, and ensuring transparency and accountability across the organization.

**TL;DR:** AI agent sprawl is a growing concern for CIOs, who must develop governance strategies to manage the risks associated with unmonitored and autonomous AI agents.
